Tribute to Wangari Maathai

      1984: Received the Right Livelihood Award for her environmental activism.
      1991: Awarded the Goldman Environmental Prize for her efforts in environmental conservation.
      2002: Elected as a Member of Parliament for Tetu Constituency and appointed Assistant Minister for Environment and Natural Resources.
      2004: Became the first African woman to win the Nobel Peace Prize for her contributions to sustainable development, democracy, and peace.
      2006: Elected as the first president of the African Union's Economic, Social and Cultural Council.
      2011: Died on September 25 from complications of ovarian cancer.
